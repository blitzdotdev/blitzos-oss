Questions, answered.

What is BlitzOS?
A macOS dynamic-island agent OS. It lives in your Mac's notch as a small bar that expands into a full chat the moment you need it. It perceives what you're doing, decides what you need, and acts for you across your real apps — the same island you see cycling through live states in the demo above, except every state is real agent work.
Does it need access to my accounts or API tokens?
No tokens, no integrations to set up. BlitzOS works your real, already-logged-in apps through your own browser, exactly as you would. There's nothing to connect and no keys to paste — if you're signed in, your agent can act there.
Which apps does it work with?
Any app you use in your browser while logged in — Gmail, your calendar, and the rest of your real, day-to-day tools. Because it drives your own logged-in sessions rather than wiring up per-app integrations, you're not limited to a fixed list. It also runs persistent terminals for builds, tests, and long-running jobs that survive a restart.
Is it safe? Do I stay in control?
You hold the veto. BlitzOS acts proactively, but any action that reaches outward is one tap from your approval — nothing leaves your hands without it. The island keeps work visible in the notch (a live status like "staged 3 drafts in Gmail, reading your calendar") so you always see what it's doing before you approve.
What can it actually do for me?
Run several independent agents at once, each its own chat thread working in parallel. Fan a hard job across many agents and track each piece on a live kanban board. Keep real terminals running for builds and tests. And anything you decide to keep becomes a live web app with its own URL that redeploys on every save — so the work ships, not just sits in a chat.
